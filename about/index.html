Why ClarityForge Exists

The energy sector has an infrastructure problem. The people who understand the grid, who've spent their careers in procurement and policy, who can actually explain what's happening with transmission, renewables, natural gas, and the markets that connect them. Those people are almost entirely absent from the public conversation.

Not because they don't have something to say. Because nobody built them the system to say it consistently, in the places where attention lives, with the production quality and distribution reach their expertise deserves.

ClarityForge was built to solve that problem. We work with a small number of energy leaders and organizations at a time because the work requires depth, not volume. Every engagement is built around a system that compounds: podcast production, executive visibility, content distribution, and institutional amplification.

Our Principles

Credibility is earned, not claimed. Every piece of content we produce reflects the expertise of the person behind it.

Consistency beats virality. A trusted voice that shows up every week builds more influence than a single post that goes viral and is forgotten.

Systems outlast campaigns. We don't build content calendars. We build infrastructure that runs without you thinking about it.

The energy sector deserves better media. The people shaping energy policy, building the grid, and making markets work should be the ones leading the public conversation about it.

Meet the Founder

Nate Peavey

Founder, ClarityForge Studios. Based in Bend, Oregon.

I started ClarityForge because I watched a pattern repeat itself across the energy sector for years. The people with the deepest expertise had the least visibility. The barrier wasn't talent or commitment. It was systems.

Before ClarityForge, I spent years building media infrastructure for organizations and founders across multiple sectors. Learning what makes content compound, how distribution actually works, and why most experts burn out trying to do it alone. When I started working with energy leaders, I found the sector where the gap was widest and the opportunity to make a difference was greatest.

Today, the ClarityForge team produces podcasts reaching 12,000+ newsletter subscribers across 115 countries, builds executive visibility roadmaps, coordinates distribution across seven platforms, and maintains a journalism-grade editorial standard on every piece of written content. I focus on strategy and client relationships. The production team handles the rest.
