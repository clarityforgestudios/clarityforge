What's inside a Foundation Build?
Strategic infrastructure
  • Audience definition brief
  • Content territory map (4-6 recurring themes)
  • Series architecture and standardized episode structure
  • Messaging positioning memo
  • Multi-voice profiles for each contributor + organizational voice layer
  • Distribution playbook (cadence, tagging, cross-voice amplification)
  • 90-day launch plan aligned to tentpole moments
Visual infrastructure
  • Series cover art (YouTube + LinkedIn compatible)
  • Custom thumbnail system (3-5 reusable templates)
  • Branded intro/outro motion graphics
  • Lower thirds template
  • Caption styling system
  • LinkedIn clip visual formatting
  • YouTube channel banner refinement (if needed)

Common questions

Can we skip the Foundation Build and start with a monthly retainer?
Only if you already have the architecture documented — audience brief, topic map, voice profiles per contributor, distribution playbook. Step 1 of the explorer asks about this directly. If you have it, we review it in the working session and start with a short onboarding instead. If you don't, we scope Foundation Build together and it kicks off the engagement.
What counts as a public-facing contributor?
Anyone at your organization who publishes under their own name, or plans to — on video, Substack, LinkedIn, research reports, or conference stages. Role titles don't matter much; willingness to show up on camera does. If three or more of your people fit that description, this page is for you.
How is this different from the podcast production page?
Podcast production is a monthly system for one voice — a solo expert or a single-leader organization. This is the multi-voice model: several contributors coordinated under one shared architecture, with Foundation Build up front that makes coordination possible. Different structure, different pricing, different deliverables. If you're a one-voice show, start there.
What does a coordinated video series actually produce?
Four things that compound over time. Talent and funder attraction — the people who could join you or fund you choose organizations with visible voices over organizations with better About pages. Policy and press influence — regulators, program officers, and journalists call the organization whose people they already see and trust. Sector recognition — your organization becomes known in the conversations where partnerships, grants, and policy decisions happen. Partnership leverage — strategic partners and coalition leads reach out because your credibility is visible, not just claimed. The algorithm works in your favor here: personal profiles get roughly 5x more organic reach than company pages, so investing in your contributors' visibility is a higher-leverage move than investing in the organization's own page.
What does the Foundation Build actually cost?
Scoped on a call. It depends on how many contributors you're profiling (3 vs. 8), how much motion graphics work you need, and how deep the positioning documentation has to go. We walk through your setup in the working session and give you a specific number before anything else moves. No commitment at that session.
What's the typical engagement length?
12 months is standard. Foundation Build is the 2-4 week kickoff; the retainer runs month to month after that with a quarterly review point. Most partnerships continue past 12 months because the compounding shows up around month 6-9 and neither side wants to break the rhythm.
How much time do our contributors actually spend on this?
30-45 minutes per recording session, plus a quick review pass. We handle strategy, production, copywriting, distribution, and measurement. That's typically 15-20 hours of work per contributor per month that doesn't land on your team.
Do you work with organizations outside of energy?
Energy is the focus. The model works for any mission-funded or institutional organization with multiple public-facing voices, but our proof points and network are in energy, policy, and climate. If you're adjacent — climate finance, energy workforce, grid infrastructure, environmental policy, public-interest research — that's a fit. Further outside the sector, we'll tell you honestly if we're the right partner.
What about the co-investment model?
For budget-constrained nonprofits and associations, we structure a 50/50 co-investment: a foundation, funder, or strategic partner covers half, your organization covers the other half. Same deliverables, lower barrier. We walk through the details in the working session if it's a fit.
