What's In Scope

Four areas of work, one operator.

You record. From there, the operation runs through ClarityForge. Strategic calls about Volts as a product stay yours. The strategic work around the business of Volts is what I run.

01
Production
  • Full audio production from raw recording: cleanup, theme music, mid-roll insertion, dual versions for free and paid feeds.
  • Transcription with hand correction. Episode page publishing on Substack.
  • YouTube upload with proper titles, chapters, thumbnails, and SEO-aware show notes.
  • Two to three short clips per episode with captions written in your voice, posted on a scheduled cadence to YouTube, Instagram, and LinkedIn.
02
Audience Development
  • Rolling back-catalog YouTube and search optimization so old episodes keep producing.
  • One themed back-catalog bundle promotion per month.
  • Searchable archive infrastructure buildout.
  • Paid-subscriber conversion strategy focused on the existing free list.
  • Marketing-army referral structure designed and piloted. Monthly metrics report you can read in two minutes.
  • Community thread coordination, comment monitoring, and routine subscriber support absorbed into the operation.
03
Narrative Strategy
  • Rules-and-guardrails framework so other voices can operate inside the Volts brand without you having to police it.
  • Editorial consistency framework. Voice profile document for anyone working on Volts content, including AI tools.
  • Light coordination on collaborations and guest essays as opportunities surface.
04
Operating Cadence
  • Single point of contact. One scheduled thirty-minute touchpoint per month, skippable without consequence.
  • Approval surfaces designed for five-minute decisions, not extended reviews.

Terms

Six months, then month-to-month.

  • Initial term: Six months, beginning with the first invoiced month.
  • After initial term: Month-to-month continuation. No auto-renewal.
  • Termination: Thirty-day written notice from either side, any time.
  • Invoicing: Monthly, net 15, beginning July 1, 2026.
  • Overage: Per-episode overage above 6/month invoiced with the same monthly cycle.
What's not in this proposal: The following are deliberately out of scope for the first six months. Each becomes a natural conversation as the base operation matures.
