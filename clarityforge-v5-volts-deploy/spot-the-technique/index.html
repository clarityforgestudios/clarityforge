What this is

The 18 techniques

Two categories. Eighteen patterns. The same recurring moves.

Structural fallacies are errors in argument logic. Rhetorical tactics are moves that work on the reader without engaging the argument. Both show up daily in public discourse on energy and science.

Structural Fallacies

  • False dichotomyTwo options framed as the only two
  • StrawmanMisrepresenting the opposing position
  • Ad hominemAttacking the person instead of the argument
  • Hasty generalizationBroad conclusion from a single example
  • Slippery slopePredicting a chain of worse consequences
  • Correlation-causation errorTreating co-occurrence as cause
  • False causeIdentifying the wrong cause for an outcome

Rhetorical Tactics

  • Cherry-pickingSelected data point misrepresenting the pattern
  • WhataboutismDeflecting by pointing at a different topic
  • Fake expertCredentials from one domain applied in another
  • Manufactured doubtFraming existing evidence as too uncertain to act on
  • Misleading chartVisual scale distorting what the data shows
  • Moving the goalpostsShifting standards as evidence is met
  • Appeal to natureNatural means good, unnatural means bad
  • Appeal to emotionFeeling substituted for evidence
  • BandwagonTrue because many or the right people believe it
  • EquivocationShifting the meaning of a word mid-argument
  • Appeal to traditionRight because it is how things have been done
