Energy Capital Podcast / Texas Energy & Power Media

Editorial Partners

Steve Scheibal and Elizabeth Souder-Philyaw

The Challenge

Energy Capital had strong institutional credibility and expert hosts. What it didn't have was operational infrastructure to publish consistently, repurpose content across platforms, and grow audience reach. The hosts were doing the hard part: having substantive, policy-relevant conversations. Everything else was a bottleneck.

Then the engagement got harder. The show navigated a major host transition mid-stride. A lesser production system would have stalled. The goal became not just maintaining cadence but using the transition as an opportunity to expand the platform into something bigger than a podcast.

What We Built

A full podcast-to-platform production pipeline. Every episode goes through six production phases (intake, processing, editing, clips, written content, distribution), and every episode generates 10+ pieces of content across podcast platforms, Substack, LinkedIn, YouTube, and social channels.

We also built a journalism-grade editorial standard (the Scheibal Filter) that governs all written content. Declarative sentences. No editorial commentary. Every piece of written content reads like it was produced by a newsroom, not a marketing agency.

How the Engagement Evolved

This didn't stay a production engagement. As the show grew and the platform expanded, the system grew with it. Here's what it looked like at the beginning versus what it looks like now.

Where we started

Production Partner

  • Single host pairing
  • Weekly podcast episodes
  • Audio editing and mastering
  • Basic show notes and distribution
  • Clips for LinkedIn

Where we are now

Full Media Ecosystem

  • Multiple hosts across different episode formats
  • Weekly episodes plus special reports and educational content
  • Full newsletter/Substack production to 12K+ subscribers
  • Cross-platform distribution: Substack, LinkedIn, X, Bluesky, YouTube
  • Host channel growth strategy across personal profiles
  • Journalism-grade editorial standard on all written content
  • Guest amplification and coordination system
  • Live recordings and panels at major events (SXSW, CERAWeek, industry conferences)
  • Sponsorship strategy, packaging, and landed podcast sponsorships

Not Just a Podcast

The system produces multiple content formats from the same editorial infrastructure. This is what makes it a media ecosystem, not just a show.

Weekly Episodes

Full-length conversations with energy policy experts, regulators, and industry leaders. 45-60 minutes.

Special Reports

Focused deep-dives on specific policy events, market shifts, or regulatory developments. Educational, not episodic.

Short-Form Clips

3-5 clips per episode optimized for LinkedIn, YouTube, and social. Designed to stop the scroll and drive back to the full episode.

Newsletter Editions

Every episode becomes a Substack edition to 12K+ subscribers. Editorial-grade writing, not AI-generated summaries.

LinkedIn Copy

Post copy for full episodes, clips, and guest amplification across host profiles and the show's channels.

SEO Content

Show notes, timestamps, transcripts, and blog posts designed to surface in search and drive long-tail discovery.
